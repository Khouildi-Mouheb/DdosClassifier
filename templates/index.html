Understanding DDoS Attacks

A Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt normal traffic of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming it with a flood of Internet traffic.

Real-world Impact:

In 2023, the largest DDoS attack recorded peaked at 710 million requests per second, highlighting the scale of modern threats.

Types of DDoS Attacks

Volumetric Attacks

Overwhelm bandwidth with massive traffic.

  • DNS Amplification
  • UDP Floods

Protocol Attacks

Exploit server protocol weaknesses.

  • SYN Floods
  • Ping of Death

Application Layer

Target specific applications/services.

  • HTTP Floods
  • Slowloris
